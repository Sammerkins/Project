Caffeine

C8H10N4O2

Otherwise known as Trimethylxanthine

Key

Black Sphere = Carbon Atom
White Sphere = Hydrogen Atom
Red Sphere = Oxygen Atom
Blue Sphere = Nitrogen Atom

Each Atom, Bond, and Angle are to scale meaning that
1 Centimeter = 1 Angstrom

Phase Changes

While it is natrually found in plants,
the pure form is a bitter crystalline powder
Picture from source 2

  1. At room temperature Caffeine is a solid
  2. Boiling point = 352 °F
  3. Melting point = 460 °F

Other Important Information

  • Molecular weight = 194.19 g/mol
  • Solubility= 10 to 50 mg/mL at 73° F (1)
  • Density = 1.23 at 64 °F
  • Stability = Fairly stable
  • Conductivity is low.
  • Reactivity = Caffeine is a weak base, but may respond to strong oxidizing agents

However coffee, containing caffeiene has about 40x the conductivity of tap water.

Where Caffeine is found

Caffeine is found in either the leaves or beans of over 60 plants

Shown above is a picture of the berries of the coffee plant

Medicinal Properties

Caffeine is often used as a medicinal substance, as it decreases inflamation and promotes stimulation. It is also higly addictive, however, within reasonable limits, it is still up for debate as to any benefits or negative side effects.
