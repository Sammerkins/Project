The American Dream

The American Dream has taken many shapes since the land was discocered in 1492. Each takes its unique place among the people's hearts as a definition of success. Depending on the time, place, and people, this goal looks different from every dream before it. However the constant is a hope for a better future, for them and for their children. Here, we will travel through many centuries to discover what the American Dream meant to different people, viewed through three classic works of literature: A Raisin in the Sun, The Life of Frederick Douglass, and Of Plymouth Plantation.

Conclusion

The American dream has evolved and adapted over the last four centuries. To this day, the constant has been providing a better future for future generations. That those future generations may stand on the worries and struggles of now and grow beyond them. This is what has driven America to now. Hopefully, it will continue to drive us into the future of tomorrow.
