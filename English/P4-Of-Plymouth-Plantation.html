Of Plymouth Plantation

And
The American Dream

Historical Background

Before America was formed as a nation, the Pilgrims looked to the hope of a better future and traveled to the new world. They “lived as people who imagined a destiny for themselves” (Cullen). This means they paved the way for the American dream to be a possibility by laying down the groundwork. Leaving England to be able to worship God in their fashion, they followed this dream to Holland. Then after living there for some time, they left Holland and sought a new world. They wanted to create a better future for their children, enabling them to pursue life, liberty, and happiness in their fashion. This was over a century before those words were used to inspire a revolution following that same dream. They landed in December of 1620 where they began to establish their colony.

Their Dream

The Pilgrims' “American dream” was to give their children a better future. To them, this meant risking everything so their children could grow up being able to worship God.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Pilgrims began the American dream. As Governor Roger Wilcott said in Of Plymouth Plantation, “For here not alone did godly men and women suffer greatly for a great cause but their noble purpose was not doomed to defeat but was carried to perfect victory. They [e]stablished what they planned. Their feeble plantation became the birthplace of religious liberty, the cradle of a free commonwealth. to them a mighty nation owns its debt” (8). We owe them a great deal for starting this dream on which the nation was built.
